Mobile Tech RX vs Urable — which detailer software should you actually pick?

Two of the most common CRMs for mobile car detailers. They look similar in the marketing. They price completely differently. Here's how to know which one fits your business.

Comparison Updated July 2026 · ~7 min read

If you've spent any time on r/AutoDetailing or in detailer Facebook groups, you've seen both names come up: Mobile Tech RX and Urable. Both promise to replace the ugly Google Sheet you're using now. Both handle estimates, invoicing, scheduling, and customer records. Both have real, working mobile apps.

But the second you look at how they charge for a team, they split hard in opposite directions. This post is the comparison we wish we'd had before we started poking at both. Pricing is pulled straight from each vendor's public pricing page as of July 2026 — verify at the source before you sign up, because SaaS pricing changes.

The 30-second version

Solo detailer, budget matters: Mobile Tech RX at $39/mo (Getting Started) is the cheapest way in.

2+ techs on your crew: Urable at $70/mo — because it charges a flat rate with unlimited users while MTX adds $15–$29/mo per person.

You do more than just detailing (PDR, glass, wheel repair, PPF, tint): Mobile Tech RX. It's built for the whole auto-recon world and has pricing packages already loaded for each.

You want the newer, less-cluttered UI: Urable feels like a modern app. MTX feels like industry-standard software from a decade ago that has kept adding features.

Mobile Tech RX — the veteran

Mobile Tech RX has been around long enough to be the default answer when someone in a Facebook group asks "what software do you use?" The company built its reputation in auto reconditioning generally — PDR shops, glass techs, wheel-rim guys — and detailing is one of several service modules. The upside of that history is depth: pre-loaded pricing packages for common services, decent estimator, real reporting, and an actual support team that answers.

The downside is where the money goes. MTX prices the primary user (the "admin") starting at $39/mo on the Getting Started plan — genuinely cheap for a solo operator. But if you bring a second tech onto the account, you add $15/mo for them on Getting Started, $29/mo on Standard, and $29/mo on Pro. That adds up quickly for a growing crew.

The three MTX tiers, decoded

Getting Started — $39/mo admin + $15/mo per extra user

One service module choice, 50 text alerts/month, credit card processing, unlimited estimates and invoices, scheduling, before/after photos, basic reporting.

For: a solo detailer who wants to move off spreadsheets and just needs the basics.

Standard — $99/mo admin + $29/mo per extra user

Everything in Getting Started plus QuickBooks Online integration, client portal, tech pay tracking, subcontracting, customer statements, deposits and discounts on estimates, 100 text alerts.

For: a detailer who has 1–2 techs, is doing enough revenue that QuickBooks matters, and wants deposit/discount handling on estimates.

Pro — $199/mo admin + $29/mo per extra user

Everything in Standard plus unlimited text alerts, expanded QuickBooks integration, check-in and QA checklists, comparative pricing, black book vehicle values, workflow management, time tracking, COGS tracking, advanced reporting, Zapier integration, premium support.

For: established shops that run multiple recon service lines, want workflow enforcement, and need cost-of-goods visibility per job.

Urable — the newer, flatter one

Urable is a younger product built for detail-specific and coating-adjacent work — think detail, PPF, tint, ceramic. It doesn't try to be the Swiss Army knife for the whole auto-recon industry. What it does differently is the pricing model: every tier includes unlimited users. There is no "add another tech, pay another line item" math.

That flip changes who Urable is a good deal for. At $70/mo Express, a solo detailer is paying nearly double what MTX charges. But bring on your first tech and MTX Standard is $99 + $29 = $128/mo; Urable Pro is $110/mo flat. Add a second tech and MTX Standard climbs to $157/mo; Urable Pro is still $110. By your third crew member, Urable Enterprise ($183/mo) is either matching or beating any MTX tier that has meaningful features.

The three Urable tiers, decoded

Express — $70/mo, unlimited users

Automated reminder messaging, cloud storage, employee job assignment, Stripe and Square integration, text/email invoicing, inventory tracking, live chat support, analytics.

For: a crew that has more than one hand on the tools and doesn't want per-user pricing yet.

Pro — $110/mo, unlimited users

Everything in Express plus custom lead forms, online booking, long-term follow-up messaging, document storage, Urable Marketplace access, drag-and-drop rescheduling, QuickBooks integration, profit analytics.

For: a growing detail or coating shop that wants a real online booking flow and QuickBooks in the mix.

Enterprise — $183/mo, unlimited users

Everything in Pro plus recurring billing, video inspections, customizable workflows, automated payment reminders, commission tracking, 1:1 account executive access, line-item scheduling.

For: multi-tech shops with commission-based pay structures and workflow-heavy service lines like PPF and coatings.

Which should you pick?

Pick Mobile Tech RX if:

  • You're solo and staying solo for the foreseeable future — the $39 Getting Started plan is genuinely a good deal.
  • Your business does more than detailing — PDR, glass, wheel repair, PPF, or tint. MTX's pre-loaded pricing packages for each service line are the closest thing to industry-standard estimating templates in this space.
  • You want in-house payment processing rather than being routed through Stripe or Square.
  • You value having a long-established company with a support team you can actually reach.

Pick Urable if:

  • You already have — or plan to have — more than one person doing the work. The unlimited-users pricing eats the per-user model alive at every crew size above one.
  • Your service mix is detail, PPF, tint, or ceramic coating. Urable is designed around exactly these workflows.
  • You want the newer, less-cluttered UI. Both apps get the job done; Urable feels lighter.
  • You want online booking baked in without third-party integrations (available on Pro).

The trap to avoid

Do not pick based on the starting price alone. If you're going to hire — even one tech, part-time — run the math on both platforms at the crew size you actually expect to hit in 12 months. The gap between MTX Standard for a 3-person crew ($157/mo) and Urable Pro for the same ($110/mo) is nearly $600/year, or roughly one extra full detail per month that has to come out of somewhere.

And do not pick based on features alone either. Both products cover the working detailer's needs: estimates, invoices, scheduling, payments, customer records, photos. The differences are in where features live in each tier, which is why the tier-by-tier tables above matter more than a raw feature checklist.

FAQ

Does Mobile Tech RX have a free trial?

Yes — 5 days, no credit card required.

Does Urable have a free trial?

Yes — Urable's site advertises "Get Started For Free" on all plans.

Is there a cheaper alternative to both?

If you're at the "just moved off spreadsheets" stage, a free stack of Wave (invoicing) + Google Calendar + Square Appointments can carry you a surprisingly long way. We're writing a separate piece on the $0 starter stack — check back for it.

Can I switch from one to the other later?

Yes, though it takes work. Both platforms let you export your client list, and neither one holds your data hostage. Expect a weekend of manual reconciliation if you migrate.
